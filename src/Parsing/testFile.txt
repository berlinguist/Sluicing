Along the storied hallways of the United States Capitol, Senator Tim Scott moves quietly, often disappearing behind his loquacious Senate colleagues and frenetic staff.

But here in his home state of South Carolina, Mr. Scott, a Republican freshman, returned to his elemental political self Monday night: standing before scores of voters, a giant poster of Abraham Lincoln behind him, quoting Scripture (Proverbs 27:17), making fun of Representative Trey Gowdy’s hair (overly styled) and playing the unlikely role of presidential kingmaker. He put former Gov. Mike Huckabee of Arkansas through his paces in the first of Mr. Scott’s town hall-style meetings featuring Republican presidential hopefuls in a state vital to winning his party’s nomination.
